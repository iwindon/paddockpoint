4th of July Firework Safety Reminder

Please celebrate responsibly and help keep our neighborhood safe.

  • Dangers: Keep children away from active fireworks and never relight a dud.
  • Safety: Use fireworks in open areas, keep water nearby, and clean up hot debris after use.
  • Damage: Be mindful of homes, cars, dry grass, and community property that can catch fire.
  • Decibels: Loud fireworks can impact neighbors, babies, veterans, and those sensitive to noise.
  • Dogs and Pets: Bring pets indoors early and use ID tags, as loud sounds can cause panic and escapes.
  • Hours: Per Spartanburg County regulations, fireworks can continue up until 1 am Sunday morning, July 5th, 2026.
  • Illegal types: M-80s, Cherry bombs, mini bottle rockets.
  • Responsibility: Celebrate respectfully, pick up all debris, and report unsafe situations when needed.

In an emergency, call 911. For non-emergency concerns, call the Spartanburg Sheriff's Department non-emergency line at 864-596-2222.

The pool is now open! The installation of the new pool gate access system has been completed, and pool access cards are now required for entry.

New Pool Hours

  • August: 9:00 a.m. to 8:00 p.m.
  • September: 9:00 a.m. to 7:00 p.m. (or dusk, whichever occurs first)

The pool railing repair has been completed, damaged tiles have been repaired, and protective safety covers are being installed over the emergency stop button and the pool gate exit button.

Please remember that it is extremely important not to allow anyone outside of your immediate group into the pool area. We also ask that you remind your children not to use the exit button to allow others to enter the pool enclosure. These measures help ensure the security of the facility and that access remains limited to authorized residents and their guests.

Any homeowner found allowing unauthorized individuals into the pool area or otherwise violating the pool access rules may have their pool access privileges suspended for the remainder of the pool season.
